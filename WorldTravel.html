PACKING AND TRAVELLING HACKS

1. Wear the heavier clothing on the flight

Wearing more of your heavier items on the flight will allow you to have more room in your bag



2. Packing cubes

You can compartimalize your suitcase with packing cubes that will keep your shirts, pants, and accessories separate. You can find some here on Amazon.



3. Roll your clothes

Rolling your clothes rather than folding them allows for more space in your suitcase for more clothes or accessories and will eliminate wrinkles.



4. Always pack an empty water bottle

Once you get through security, you can fill your water bottle at a water fountain to avoid having to pay money every time you need water.

SPAIN

Madrid, the capital of Spain, is a cosmopolitan city that combines the most modern infrastructures and the status as an economic, financial, administrative and service centre, with a large cultural and artistic heritage, a legacy of centuries of exciting history. Strategically located in the geographic centre of the Iberian Peninsula at an altitude of 646 m above sea level, Madrid has one of the most important historic centres of all the great European cities. This heritage merges seamlessly with the city's modern and convenient infrastructures, a wide-ranging offer of accommodation and services, and all the latest state-of-the-art technologies in audiovisual and communications media. These conditions, together with all the drive of a dynamic and open society –as well as high-spirited and friendly– have made this metropolis one of the great capitals of the Western world.

TOP THINGS TO DO IN SPAIN:

Royal Palace of Madrid:
The Royal Palace is one of the top tourist attractions in Madrid. It is the official residence for the Royal family, but these days they only use it for state ceremonies. The rest of the time its open to the public. Otherwise known in Spanish as the Palacio Real, the Royal Palace was built during the 18th and 19th centuries, and is a monumental building. Click here to buy tickets!

Museo del Prado:
The Prado has one of the largest art collections in the world, and is best known for its diverse assortment of works by Velasquez, Goya and El Greco. Click here to buy tickets!
Plaza Mayor:
The Plaza Mayor, a grand arcaded square in the center of Madrid is very popular with tourists and locals alike.The symmetrical rectangular square features a uniform architecture. The name of the plaza has changed over time. Originally it was called the "Plaza del Arrabal" but became known as the "Plaza Mayor". It's free to roam around and there are restaurants around the plaza to eat.
Puerta del Sol:
Puerta del Sol’s third and largest statue is found in the centre of the square and depicts King Charles III of Spain on horseback. During his reign, the monarch introduced so many progressive reforms and gave the city such a makeover that he earned the moniker Madrid’s best mayor.The square also contains the famous clock whose bells mark the traditional eating of the Twelve Grapes and the beginning of a new year. The Seville Cathedral is the largest gothic Cathedral in the world. It was built to demonstrate the city's wealth, as it had become a major trading center in the years after the Reconquista in 1248. Parque Guell
Park Guell is one of the most beautiful icons of the city. It's a work of Antoni Gaudí located in the upper part of Barcelona, which has wonderful views of the city. Aqueduct of Segovia
The Aqueduct of Segovia was built during the second half of the 1st century A.D. under the rule of the Roman Empire and supplied water from the Frío River to the city into the 20th century. Historically and culturally among the most important nations in the Western world, France has also played a highly significant role in international affairs, with former colonies in every corner of the globe. Bounded by the Atlantic Ocean and the Mediterranean Sea, the Alps and the Pyrenees, France has long provided a geographic, economic, and linguistic bridge joining northern and southern Europe. It is Europe’s most important agricultural producer and one of the world’s leading industrial powers. There are many things to do in France

TOP THINGS TO DO IN FRANCE:

Eiffel Tower
In 1889, Paris hosted an World’s Fair to mark the 100-year anniversary of the French Revolution. More than 100 artists submitted competing plans for a monument to be built on the Champ-de-Mars, located in central Paris, and serve as the exposition’s entrance. The commission was granted to Eiffel et Compagnie, a consulting and construction firm owned by the acclaimed bridge builder, architect and metals expert Alexandre-Gustave Eiffel. Click here to visit the tower!
Louvre Museum
The Louvre was originally built as a fortress in 1190, but was reconstructed in the 16th century to serve as a royal palace. It is the world's largest art museum. It is home to the Mona Lisa, the Code of Hammurabi, and the Venus de Milo. It has a collection that spans work from ancient civilizations to the mid-19th century. Click here to buy your tickets!
Arc de Triomphe
Arc de Triomphe is a massive triumphal arch and one of the world’s best-known commemorative monuments. It stands at the centre of the Place Charles de Gaulle, the western terminus of the avenue des Champs-Élysées. Napoleon I commissioned the triumphal arch in 1806—after his great victory at the Battle of Austerlitz —to celebrate the military achievements of the French armies. The arch, designed by Jean-François-Thérèse Chalgrin, is 164 feet high and 148 feet wide. It sits in a circular plaza from which 12 grand avenues radiate, forming a star. Prepare for your visit by checking this website out!
Palace of Versailles
Palace of Versailles, former French royal residence and centre of government, now a national landmark. It is located in the city of Versailles, Yvelines department, Île-de-France region, northern France, 10 miles west-southwest of Paris. As the centre of the French court, Versailles was one of the grandest theatres of European absolutism. Click here to get your tickets!
French Rivera
The French Riviera, also known as the Côte d’Azur, is a dreamy French region that extends east along the coast from Menton and Monaco to Théoule sur Mer and up into the Southern Alps. The Riviera contains several cities (Nice and Cannes among them), 14 natural parks, Roman ruins, medieval villages and whale watching just off shore.
Mont Blanc
Mont Blanc is the highest mountain in the Alps and the highest in Europe west of the Caucasus peaks of Russia and Georgia. It rises 4,808 m above sea level and is ranked 11th in the world in topographic prominence. The mountain stands in a range called the Graian Alps, between the regions of Aosta Valley, Italy, and Savoie and Haute-Savoie, France.

ITALY

Italy occupies a peninsula that juts deep into the Mediterranean Sea. Italy is often described as a country shaped like a boot. At its broad top stand the Alps, which are among the world’s most rugged mountains. Italy’s highest points are along Monte Rosa, which peaks in Switzerland, and along Mont Blanc, which peaks in France. The western Alps overlook a landscape of Alpine lakes and glacier-carved valleys that stretch down to the Po River and the Piedmont. Tuscany, to the south of the cisalpine region, is perhaps the country’s best-known region. South of Rome the Apennines narrow and are flanked by two wide coastal plains, one facing the Tyrrhenian Sea and the other the Adriatic Sea. Much of the lower Apennine chain is near-wilderness, hosting a wide range of species rarely seen elsewhere in western Europe, such as wild boars, wolves, asps, and bears. The southern Apennines are also tectonically unstable, with several active volcanoes, including Vesuvius, which from time to time belches ash and steam into the air above Naples and its island-strewn bay. At the bottom of the country, in the Mediterranean Sea, lie the islands of Sicily and Sardinia.

TOP THINGS TO DO IN ITALY:

St. Peter's Basilica
St. Peter’s Basilica present basilica of St. Peter in Vatican City (an enclave in Rome), begun by Pope Julius II in 1506 and completed in 1615 under Paul V. It is designed as a three-aisled Latin cross with a dome at the crossing, directly above the high altar, which covers the shrine of St. Peter the Apostle. The edifice—the church of the popes—is a major pilgrimage site. Click here to visit the museum!
Roman Colosseum
The construction of the Colosseum was begun in 72 CE in the reign of Vespasian on the site that was once the lake and gardens of Emperor Nero’s Golden House. The Roman Colosseum is the most famous monument to have survived from the classical world. It was built nearly two thousand years ago for the purpose of hosting violent gladiator games. Thousands of men and animals fought for their lives in the sandy arena. Click here to buy your tickets!
Trevi Fountain
Trevi Fountain is the most beautiful fountain in Rome.Measuring some 20 meters in width by 26 meters in height, Trevi Fountain is also the largest fountain in the city.The origins of the fountain go back to the year 19 B.C., in which period the fountain formed the end of the Aqua Virgo aqueduct.The first fountain was built during the Renaissance, under the direction of Pope Nicholas V.
Galleria Borghese
The Galleria Borghese is housed in the Villa Borghese. It is one of the most renowned art museums in the world. The building’s gardens are also a popular attraction and the third largest park in Rome. Click here to visit the museum!
Roman Forum
The Roman Forum was a site located at the center of the ancient city of Rome and the location of important religious, political and social activities. Historians believe people first began publicly meeting in the open-air Forum around 500 B.C., when the Roman Republic was founded. The rectangular-shaped area, sited on low-lying land between Palatine Hill and Capitoline Hill, was home to many of the ancient city’s most impressive temples and monuments. Today, the Roman Forum is one of the most famous tourist sites in the world, attracting more than 4.5 million. Buy your tickets here for a bundle!
Sistine Chapel
The chapel’s exterior is drab and unadorned, but its interior walls and ceiling are decorated with frescoes by many Florentine Renaissance masters. The frescoes on the side walls of the chapel were painted from 1481 to 1483.The most important artworks in the chapel are the frescoes by Michelangelo on the ceiling and on the west wall behind the altar. The frescoes on the ceiling, collectively known as the Sistine Ceiling, were commissioned by Pope Julius II in 1508 and were painted by Michelangelo in the years from 1508 to 1512. They depict incidents and personages from the Old Testament. The Last Judgment fresco on the west wall was painted by Michelangelo for Pope Paul III in the period from 1534 to 1541. These two gigantic frescoes are among the greatest achievements of Western painting. A 10-year-long cleaning and restoration of the Sistine Ceiling completed in 1989 removed several centuries’ accumulation of dirt, smoke, and varnish. Cleaning and restoration of the Last Judgment was completed in 1994. Click here to get your tickets!

Rialto Bridge, stone-arch bridge crossing over the narrowest point of the Grand Canal in the heart of Venice. Built in the closing years of the 16th century, the Rialto Bridge is the oldest bridge across the canal and is renowned as an architectural and engineering achievements of the Renaissance. It was designed and built by Antonio da Ponte and his nephew, Antonio Contino, following a design competition in the city.

It is one of the most striking squares in the world, a masterful expression of human creativity. Since 1982 it has been a UNESCO World Heritage Site. THINGS TO DO IN PERU:

Machu Picchu
Machu Picchu is an Incan citadel set high in the Andes Mountains in Peru, above the Urubamba River valley. Built in the 15th century and later abandoned, it’s renowned for its sophisticated dry-stone walls that fuse huge blocks without the use of mortar, intriguing buildings that play on astronomical alignments and panoramic views. Its exact former use remains a mystery. You can buy your tickets online and in Cusco.
Nazca Lines
The Nazca Lines are a collection of giant geoglyphs—designs or motifs etched into the ground—located in the Peruvian coastal plain about 250 miles south of Lima, Peru. Created by the ancient Nazca culture in South America, and depicting various plants, animals, and shapes, the 2,000-year-old Nazca Lines can only be fully appreciated when viewed from the air given their massive size. Despite being studied for over 80 years, the geoglyphs are still a mystery to researchers.
Rainbow Mountain
Montaña de Colores or Rainbow Mountain, is a mountain in Peru with an altitude of 5,200 meters above sea level. Tourist access requires a two-hour drive from Cusco, and a walk of about 5 kilometres.
Lake Titicaca
Lake Titicaca the world’s highest lake navigable to large vessels, lying at 12,500 feet above sea level in the Andes Mountains of South America, astride the border between Peru to the west and Bolivia to the east. Titicaca is the second largest lake of South America. It covers some 3,200 square miles and extends in a northwest-to-southeast direction for a distance of 120 miles.
Huacachina
Huacachina is a desert oasis and tiny village just west of the city of Ica in southwestern Peru. At its center are the green waters of the Huacachina Lagoon, ringed by palm trees and thought to have therapeutic properties. Dune buggies run across the high, rolling sand dunes surrounding the village.
Islas Ballestas
The Ballestas Islands lie off the southern coast of Peru, near the city of Pisco. Rich in marine life, the uninhabited islands are home to sea lions, pelicans, Peruvian boobies and Humboldt penguins. Small boats run from Pisco and the nearby town of Paracas out to the islands' craggy rock formations. On the way, they pass the Paracas Candelabra geoglyph, an enormous hillside etching of mysterious origins.

SOUTH AFRICA

The Western Cape offers beaches, lush winelands around Stellenbosch and Paarl, craggy cliffs at the Cape of Good Hope, forest and lagoons along the Garden Route, and the city of Cape Town, beneath flat-topped Table Mountain. Located in the extreme southern part of the African continent, South Africa makes a unique tourist destination. Home to one of the most diverse landscapes, abundant wildlife and world renowned cities, these are some great reasons to visit South Africa, any visitor will be amazed by what the country has to offer.

Johannesburg:
Johannesburg, or Jo’burg, is the economic heart of Africa and the most common entry point into Southern Africa. With more than 3 million people it is also the second largest city on the continent. Historically it is where money is made and fortunes found. It has been stereotyped as a cruel, concrete jungle, plagued by crime, but residents defend it fiercely as a city of opportunity and raw energy.


Addo Elephant National Park:
Located in the Eastern Cape region of South Africa, the Addo Elephant National Park is one of the country’s larger parks. The Park is famous for its elephant population that has a special, brownish skin color due to the red soil. Amongst the elephants other animals, like ostriches, antelopes and warthogs can be seen.

Ethiopia

Ethiopia, in the Horn of Africa, is a rugged, landlocked country split by the Great Rift Valley. With archaeological finds dating back more than 3 million years, it’s a place of ancient culture. Among its important sites are Lalibela with its rock-cut Christian churches from the 12th–13th centuries.Showcasing ancient history, picturesque landscapes, joyful festivals, and much more, Ethiopia has plenty of choices in store for the curious traveler. Its unique calendar, old alphabet, and numerical system are some of the things that set the country apart.




THE MUSIC

In the 60s and 70s, Ethiopia became known for its own brand of jazz, but the music scene was entirely shut down during the communist regime that lasted from 1974 until the early 90s. Since then it's made a comeback, and Addis is now an excellent place to hear jazz and other live music.




GREAT SHOPPING

Ethiopia has a long tradition of beautiful handicrafts including high-quality hand-woven and embroidered materials. You can also find leather goods like sandals and hats. Addis is such a fashionable place that it recently hosted a fashion week, Hub of Africa, showcasing the works of Ethiopian and other African-born designers.




MOROCCO

Morocco, a North African country bordering the Atlantic Ocean and Mediterranean Sea, is distinguished by its Berber, Arabian and European cultural influences. North Africa’s Morocco is a popular destination, attracting culture lovers, backpackers, adventure travelers, couples, families, foodies, and more.




THE CULTURE

In Essaouira, a city in Morocco you will hear the call of prayer from the Mosque, see the men dressed in traditional clothing, the women too. You may not understand the menu and exactly what people are saying (unless you speak French), you will probably see sights in the food markets that perhaps you don’t want to see but this is all part of indulging in the Culture and observing everyday life.




THE ARCHITECTURE

The cultural diversity of Morocco is reflected in the varied architectural beauties that can be found all across the nation. From majestic mosques to traditional riads and from brilliant palaces to sublime centers of religious study, Morocco has many fabulous buildings to admire. Many buildings feature the traditional tilework, known as zellige, as well as decorative carvings, woodwork, and plasterwork.




TOP PLACES TO GO IN MOROCCO

The Ramparts:
The symbol of the red city, the ramparts which construction, commissioned by ali Ben Youssef, began in 1126, surround the old town today: the medina. it took decades to build the ramparts so that the adobe and clay-made walls would reach more than 10 km. Those walls are interspersed by 202 square –shaped towers and 10 imposing gates give access to the town.


La Sqala
La Sqala is one of the loveliest places to get a taste of the ocean. Diners sit in a leafy gazebo garden near the 18th-century Portuguese sea walls, and order the catch of the day for lunch. It's also possible to while away an hour over coffee and fresh juices.

CHINA

China is considered to be the oldest civilization with some historians marking 6000 BC as the beginning of the Chinese civilization. Also, it has the world's longest used written language. As of July 2012, China has the largest population in the world with over 1.3 billion people.



Where to visit in China:


The Great Wall of China:
The Great Wall of China stretches more than 6,000 kilometers from the fortress of Shanhaiguan in the east all the way to Jiayuguan in the west, passing through Hebei, Tientsin, Beijing, Inner Mongolia, Ningxia, and Gansu.

The Forbidden City and the Imperial Palace, Beijing:
China's largest and most important building, the Forbidden City — also known as the Imperial Palace — is situated in the very heart of Beijing and is a must-see when visiting the country. The Temple of Heaven is also located in this vicinity, it is known that the emperors were also known as the sons of heaven as it was believed that emperors received their power from Heaven.

The Li River:
The Li River is located on the Northeast corner of Guangxi, known to be China’s most beautiful countryside. The best way to enjoy the river is by taking a cruise.

The Classic Gardens of Suzhou:
Known to be the world’s most important historic gardens. The magnificent gardens were established in the 11th century, at a time when the city was experiencing unprecedented growth.

Hong Kong Skyline:
Hong Kong is known to have the highest concentrations of skyscrapers in the world. One of the best views are from Victoria Peak on Hong Kong Island, where the skyscrapers frame the city’s huge harbor.

Japan

Japan is an island country in East Asia. Located in the Pacific Ocean, it lies off the eastern coast of the Asian continent and stretches from the Sea of Okhotsk in the north to the East China Sea and the Philippine Sea in the south.



Where to visit in Japan:


Mount Fuji:
It is known to be the country’s highest mountain peak, towering 3,776 meters over a largely flat landscape to the south and east. Mount Fuji is climbed by more than a million people each summer as an act of pilgrimage.

Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park:
It was built to commemorate those who died due to the atomic bombing of Hiroshima and Nagasaki in August 1945. It is visited by more than a million people each year, many who are from overseas.

Temple City(Historic Nara):
The city of Nara is known to be home to a large number of historic buildings, along with important nation treasures and works of art. It consists of Seven Great Temples, one known to be called Todai-ji (Great East Temple). Todai-ji is famous for its Great South Gate and the Hall of the Great Buddha.

Osaka Castle:
Built in 1586 by a famous warrior and politician Toyotomi Hideyoshi. It was the largest and most important fortress in the country. A popular interest in the Osaka Castle Park is the Hokoku Shrine as it is known to Japan’s first Buddhist temple. The shrine features a five-story pagoda along with a number of exquisitely decorated buildings including the Golden Pavilion.

Mount Yoshino:
Covered in over 30,000 cherry trees, Mount Yoshino is known to be Japan’s most popular cherry blossom viewing spots for centuries. The best time to visit and view the cherry blossom is from April to early May.

Thailand

Thailand is a Southeast Asian country. It's known for tropical beaches, opulent royal palaces, ancient ruins and ornate temples displaying figures of Buddha. In Bangkok, the capital, an ultramodern cityscape rises next to quiet canalside communities and the iconic temples of Wat Arun, Wat Pho and the Emerald Buddha Temple (Wat Phra Kaew). Nearby beach resorts include bustling Pattaya and fashionable Hua Hin.



Where to visit in Thailand:


Railay Beach:
Considered to be one of the best beaches in the country. Many hikers and rock-climbers come to experience the climb of a towering limestone cliff. Among the many other active things to do, you can go elephant trekking, whitewater rafting, kayaking, and snorkeling.

The Grand Palace:
This is located in Bangkok, Thailand. It is the number one sightseeing attraction in the city, staggering in historical significance and craftsmanship. One of the most important relics in the Grand Palace is the Wat Phra Kaeo, Temple of the Emerald Buddha.

Sunday Walking Street:
On this certain day of the week, street vendors sell all kinds of treats: pad Thai, Chicken Satay, Samosas, Crab Cakes, Fried Bananas, Sweet Rotees, and Fresh fruit shakes. This is usually held during the night and one of the best ways to taste many kinds of food in a cheap way.

Khao Yai National Park:
Elephants are auspicious in Thailand as statues and paintings of elephants can be seen everywhere you go. The park allows you to view the elephants in their natural habitat, trek with them, bathe them, and feed them. The most memorable part of this trip would be experiencing the sunrise over the lush landscape.

Floating Markets:
This is the most fun way to do some shopping and eating while supporting local vendors and observing the local commerce in action. You’d need to wake up early to visit a floating market as vendors are out in the morning with their goods, fresh fruits, vegetables, spices, and tasty dishes.

Korea

Korea is a region in East Asia. Since 1948 it has been divided between two distinct sovereign states, North Korea and South Korea. Korea consists of the Korean Peninsula, Jeju Island, and several minor islands near the peninsula.



Where to visit in Korea:


Seongsan Sunrise Peak:
It is located on Jeju Island, which was formed 5,000 years ago due to hydrovolcanic eruptions. Seongsan Sunrise Peak is best known for the magnificent sight of the sun rising over it. It is a UNESCO world heritage site famous for many things, including its numerous rare plant species.

Andong:
Andong is referred to as the Capital City of Korean Spiritual Culture. The main attraction in the city is the Andong Hahoe Folk Village, where local cultural heritage has been preserved and is shared with visitors. However, the most famous event in the city has to do with food. The city boasts all kinds of local specialities, starting from famed chicken and noodle dish (Jjimdak) to their special type of alcohol called soju.

Seoul:
Seoul is the largest city in South Korea and a major destination in East Asia. Seoul is known to be the most toured city in South Korea as there are endless attractions. Top tourist picks include the incredible Gyeongbok-gung and the 15th century Changdeok-gung. Lastly, be sure to be famished by some the best cuisine in all of Asia.

Busan:
Busan is known to be the second largest city of South Korea. Busan is located on the south-east coast of South Korea, hence it is famous for its mountains and beaches. One of the most visited beaches in Busan is Haeundae Beach.

Pyeongchang:
If you love astonishing scenery and hiking, then Pyeongchang is the place for you. It is a mountain region sitting 2,300 feet above sea level. It’s known to be the perfect location for the Winter Olympics as it has a lot of seasonal snow.

Sydney

Sydney, capital of New South Wales and one of Australia's largest cities, is best known for its harbourfront Sydney Opera House, with a distinctive sail-like design. Massive Darling Harbour and the smaller Circular Quay port are hubs of waterside life, with the arched Harbour Bridge and esteemed Royal Botanic Garden nearby. Sydney Tower’s outdoor platform, the Skywalk, offers 360-degree views of the city and suburbs.



Where to visit in Sydney:


Sydney Harbour Bridge:
Sydney Harbour Bridge, also known as “Coathanger” is the city’s best-known landmark prior to construction of the Sydney Opera House. It was built in 1932 and remains the world’s largest steel arch bridge. Experience a breathtaking panorama view of the city and harbor on the bridge.

Sydney Opera House:
Known to be one of the world’s great icons. The building is shaped like shells or billowing sails, perches on a finger of land surrounded by water. Snap a photo while gliding by on a harbor cruise, relax at one of the restaurants, or take an organized tour of the magnificent structure. It encompasses theaters, studios, exhibition rooms, a concert hall, and a cinema.

Harbor Cruises from Circular Quay:
Circular Quay is home to the city’s main ferry terminal. One of the best ways to appreciate Sydney’s sparkling waterfront setting is to hop aboard on a harbor cruise. Ferries depart from the harbor to prime spots, such as Manly, Watsons Bay, and Taronga Park Zoo. During the winter season, Sydney Whale-Watching Cruise takes passengers past Sydney Heads to view the magnificent creatures.

Taronga Zoo:
At the Taronga Zoo, enjoy close-up encounters with iconic Aussie wildlife and other animals from around the world, and spectacular views of the Sydney skyline. The zoo is located along the north side of the harbor, known to be prime in the posh suburb of Mosman. Highlights at the zoo include Lemur Walk-Through, Koala Encounter, and Seal Show. To reach the Zoo you can either hop on a bus at Wynyard or hop aboard a ferry at Circular Quay.

Melbourne

Melbourne is the coastal capital of the southeastern Australian state of Victoria. At the city's centre is the modern Federation Square development, with plazas, bars, and restaurants by the Yarra River. In the Southbank area, the Melbourne Arts Precinct is the site of Arts Centre Melbourne – a performing arts complex – and the National Gallery of Victoria, with Australian and indigenous art.



Where to visit in Melbourne:


Royal Botanic Gardens:
Known to be the heart of green parkland extending south of the Yarra River. It was established in 1846, the gardens encompass two locations: MMelbourne and Cranbourne. The Melbourne Gardens covers an area of 38 hectares with more than 8,500 species of plants.The Aboriginal Heritage Walk is held to look into the rich heritage of indigenous Australians. It is a popular spot for a picnic by the lake or a traditional high tea at The Terrace Cafe.

Federation Square:
It was opened in 2002 to commemorate 100 years of federation. It has become an integral part of the city and a great place for tourists to start their sightseeing. As it holds more than 2,000 events annually, you can find entertainment in the central outdoor performance space and intimate indoor venues. Federation Square houses the Ian Potter Centre: NGV Australia (dedicated to Australian Art) and the Australian Centre for Moving Images (ACMI).

Melbourne Cricket Ground and the National Sports Museum:
Melbourne is the sporting capital of Australia. The Melbourne Cricket Ground (MCG) has a capacity of 100,000 and a history dating back to 1853. MCG was the main stadium for the 1956 Olympic Games and the 2006 Commonwealth Games, the birthplace of Test Cricket, and the home of Australian Rules Football.The daily 75-minute tours take visitors for a trip down a memory lane of great moments in sporting history and incorporate the National Sports Museum.

National Gallery Of Victoria:
National Gallery of Victoria is the oldest public art gallery in Australia. It holds more than 70,000 works of art in two city locations. It originally opened in 1968 and extensively renovated in 2003. The building is renowned for The Great Hall, where visitors are encouraged to lie on the floor and gaze at the colorful stained glass ceiling.

Cairns

Cairns, considered the gateway to Australia's Great Barrier Reef, is a city in tropical Far North Queensland. Its Tjapukai Aboriginal Cultural Park tells the stories of indigenous Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people with music and dance. Cairns Esplanade, lined with bars and restaurants, has a swimming lagoon. Northwest of the city, Daintree National Park spans mountainous rainforest, gorges and beaches.



Where to visit in Cairns:


Great Barrier Reef Cruises:
Take a cruise to one of Australia;s greatest treasures, the World Heritage-listed Great Barrier Reef. It stretches for more than 2,300 kilometers, the reed is one of the planet’s richest ecosystems, with a dazzling diversity of hard and soft corals and countless species of fish, molluscs, and other marine animals, including dolphins, turtles, and sharks.

Cairns Esplanade:
Cairns Esplanade is a fabulous place to enjoy a sunrise to Sunset Stroll and soak up the video of this friendly social hub. The Esplanade encomapsses lish tropical parks, playgrounds, a swimming lagoon, and foreshore promenade. Birdlife is abundant in the fringing mangroves and tidal flats. Among the treasure of free attractions are a bouldering park, beach volleyball courts, bike paths, a skate park, exercise equipment, water-themed playgrounds, and more.

Cairns Botanic Gardens:
Cairns Botanic Gardens boasts one of the finest collections of tropical plants in Australia. Botanists can take a self-guided tour through several kaleidoscopic gardens showcasing more than 4,00 different species of plants. You can also stroll along the Rainforest Boardwalk to Centenary Lakes, a heaven for birds with both freshwater and saltwater habitats.

Day Trip to Green Island:
Cloaked in rainforest and ringes by coral reefs, Green Island is a tropical island paradise a short boat ride from Cairns. Snorkeling, scuba diving, and swimming are the main activities, but for those who prefer to stay dry can enjoy the view of the marine life from a glass-bottomed boat or underwater observatory.

Gold Coast

The Gold Coast is a metropolitan region south of Brisbane on Australia’s east coast. It's famed for its long sandy beaches, surfing spots and elaborate system of inland canals and waterways. It’s also home to theme parks such as Dreamworld, Sea World and Wet’n’Wild. Inland, hiking trails crisscross Lamington National Park’s mountain ridges and valleys, home to rare birds and rainforest.



Where to visit in Gold Coast:


Surfers Paradise Beach:
Perfect vacation spot for your family. Kids can splash around in the shallows of build sandcastles in the soft white sand. Surfers can ride the typically gentle swells, and if you want a break from the sun, you can seek shelter in one of the many nearby shops, cafes, and restaurants.

SkyPoint, Surfer’s Paradise:
SkyPoint is Australia’s tallest residential tower and offers giddy views of the entire Gold Coast. The SkyPoint Observation Deck on level 77 affords 360-degree panoramas of the Gold Coast, the sea, and the distant green hinterland.

Currumbin Wildlife Sanctuary:
Currumbin Wildlife Sanctuary is a well-known wildlife park owned by the non-profit National Trust of Queensland. Bird lovers are in for a treat here. Hundreds of brightly colored rainbow lorikeets eat from visitors’ hands and even rech on their heads. Other highlights include the free-flight bird show, a Tarzan-worthy Adventure Park High Ropes Course, Segway tours, and a little train that will delight toddlers. In addition, you can commune with kangaroos, cuddle koalas and other fun activities with animals.

Sea World:
Sea World on the Gold COast is the largest marine park in Australia. Young and old alike enjoy the exhibits and busy agenda of shows here, with favorites like performing dolphins, sea lions, and Jet-Ski stunts. Other highlights include the polar bears, penguins feeding presentation, as well as the tropical fish, sharks, and rays exhibit. In addition, the park offers sea-themed rides like roller coasters.
